⚠ PHISHING
🔓 https://paypa1.com/verify

The letter L is swapped for the number 1. Always read the domain character by character before entering credentials.
📧 Spoofed Sender Address
⚠ PHISHING
From: Netflix Billing <billing@netflix-update.net>
✓ LEGITIMATE
From: Netflix <info@mailer.netflix.com>
Display names can say anything. Always check the actual domain inside the angle brackets — not just the friendly name.
⚡ Urgency & Threatening Language
⚠ PHISHING — Pressure tactics
Your account will be permanently suspended in 24 hours unless you act immediately. Do not ignore this message.
✓ LEGITIMATE — Neutral tone
A new sign-in was detected on your account. If this was you, no action is needed.
Real services give you time to respond. Panic-inducing deadlines and threats are manipulation tactics designed to bypass critical thinking.
🔑 Excessive Data Collection
✓ Email
✓ Password
⚠ SSN Last 4 Digits
⚠ Full Card Number
⚠ Card PIN
Login pages only need credentials. Any form requesting SSN, full card number, CVV, or PIN alongside your password is harvesting financial data.
👤 Generic vs Personalised Greeting
⚠ PHISHING — No personalisation
"Dear Customer,"
"Dear Valued Member,"
✓ LEGITIMATE — Knows your name
"Hi John Doe,"
"Hello john.doe@gmail.com,"
Services you use already know your name or account details. A generic greeting suggests a mass-sent phishing campaign.
🎁 Too-Good-To-Be-True Offers

You cannot win a lottery you never entered. No legitimate investment doubles your money instantly. Any "fee to receive funds" is an advance-fee scam.

    Phishing Detection Guide

    🔗 Suspicious URLs

    Look for misspelled domains (paypa1.com), extra subdomains (secure.bank.malicious.com), unusual TLDs (.xyz, .tk), or IP addresses instead of domain names.

    ⚠️ Urgency & Threats

    Phrases like "Act immediately", "Your account will be suspended", or "Verify now or lose access" are pressure tactics designed to bypass critical thinking.

    🎭 Fake Branding

    Watch for logo distortions, wrong color schemes, different fonts, or subtle name changes (Amaz0n, Netf1ix, PayPa1). Legitimate brands are consistent.

    📧 Sender Mismatch

    The display name may say "PayPal Support" but the actual email address could be support@paypal-secure.xyz. Always check the actual sender address.

    🔐 Unnecessary Data Requests

    Legitimate services rarely ask for your full SSN, CVV, password, or PIN via email or pop-up. Banks will never ask for your full card number by email.

    ✍️ Grammar & Spelling

    Professional companies proofread their communications. Unusual grammar, odd spacing, or awkward phrasing can indicate non-native attackers or automated generation.

    🎁 Unrealistic Rewards

    "You've been selected!" or "Claim your $1000 gift card now!" are classic lures. If it sounds too good to be true, it almost certainly is.

    📱 QR Code Phishing

    QR codes can redirect to malicious URLs. Always preview the URL before visiting. Be suspicious of QR codes in unexpected places or unsolicited emails.

    🔒 Misuse of HTTPS

    HTTPS means the connection is encrypted — NOT that the site is legitimate. Phishing sites commonly use HTTPS. Always verify the domain name itself.

    😨 Emotional Manipulation

    Fear, excitement, and curiosity are exploited. "Your account has been hacked", "You've won a prize", or "Someone is accessing your files" are manipulation tactics.

    📎 Suspicious Attachments

    Unexpected invoices, shipping labels, or "important documents" are common. Malware hides in .docx, .pdf, .zip, and .exe files sent from unknown senders.

    📝 Fake Login Forms

    Clone sites mimic real login pages pixel-for-pixel. Always verify the URL is the real domain before entering credentials. Use a password manager that won't autofill on fakes.
